In September 2024, a cyclone devastated over 100 municipalities in Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil, displacing thousands of families. While other tech groups were building solutions for people, I noticed a gap: hundreds of lost pets, some found over 20km from home, with no centralized way to reconnect them with their owners.

I led the development of the MVP in under 7 hours: a public portal built with Vue and TypeScript, shipped from a hotel room in São Paulo. The platform allowed anyone to register a lost or found pet, search by location, and contact owners directly.

Within the first four days: 150+ pets registered, 20+ reunited with their families, thousands of daily visits, and over 1,000 followers on Instagram.

The project attracted support from major companies: Monday.com provided a special plan through their natural disaster relief program, AWS offered server credits for several months, and OpenAI granted $1,000 in API credits to power an AI-generated sketch feature that helped identify animals from descriptions.

At its peak the site reached millions of daily visits and was officially recognized by the Rio Grande do Sul state government as part of the disaster response effort.
